I am a Master’s student in Computer Science at the University of California, Irvine, where I work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Institute for Future Health and the Hayes Lab. My research focuses on computer vision and AI systems, particularly human-centric vision, anatomically consistent segmentation, and synthetic-to-real learning.

I began in the Hayes Lab as a full-stack developer working on research infrastructure, where I was later promoted to Full Stack Lead. I then transitioned into computer vision research, focusing on large-scale synthetic data generation and human pose estimation, which led to my current graduate research roles across computer vision and AI safety.

My current work includes building large-scale synthetic human pose datasets in Unreal Engine and developing anatomically consistent human part segmentation benchmarks. I focus on data-centric approaches, including dataset design, benchmarking, and evaluation under occlusion and class imbalance. In parallel, I work on AI safety and alignment, developing evaluation and rewriting systems for sensitive domains such as mental health.


I am always open to research collaborations. Feel free to reach out!
