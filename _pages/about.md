I am a Master’s student in Computer Science at the University of California, Irvine, where I work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Institute for Future Health and the Hayes Lab. My research lies at the intersection of Computer Vision and AI Systems, with a focus on human-centric vision, anatomically consistent segmentation, and synthetic-to-real learning.

I am currently working on projects involving large-scale synthetic human pose data generation using Unreal Engine, as well as anatomically consistent human part segmentation. My work emphasizes data-centric approaches, including dataset design, benchmarking, and evaluation under challenging conditions such as occlusion and class imbalance. In parallel, I am also involved in AI safety and alignment research, focusing on evaluation and rewriting systems for sensitive domains such as mental health.

I received my Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from the University of California, Irvine, where I gained experience in full-stack development and machine learning systems. My broader interests include computer vision, deep learning, and building scalable, real-world AI systems, with a long-term goal of pursuing research in industry settings.

I am always open to research collaborations. Feel free to reach out!
